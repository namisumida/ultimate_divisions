Ultimate frisbee:
Does the game differ by division?

A comparison of throw types, success rates, offensive and defensive efficiency in competitive Men's, Women's, and Mixed ultimate frisbee

2. Offensive points

In all three divisions, the winning teams convert about eight-in-ten of all of their offensive points: 81% for Men's, 78% for Mixed, and 78% for Women's (in-line with expectations for most elite club teams).

However, many of these are not without turnovers. For the winning teams in the Women’s and Mixed divisions, about half of all offensive points are single-possession scores (i.e. without turnovers). In contrast, the winning teams in the Men’s division stand out, scoring 61% of their offensive points in one possession.

It is important to note that the chart above only shows metrics for the winning teams. The winning teams in the Women's division only include four teams: Boston Brute Squad (4), San Francisco Fury (3), Seattle Riot (2), and Columbia Revolution. Similarly, only four teams are represented in the Mixed division: Seattle Mixtape (4), Boston Slow White (3), Philadelphia AMP (2), and Metro North. In the Men's division, however, there is a somewhat wider spread of teams: San Francisco Revolver (4), Boston Ironside (2), Chicago Machine, New York PoNY, Austin Doublewide, and Seattle Sockeye.

While winning teams clearly have more offensive holds than losing teams, it may not necessarily be the case that their offensive holds are more likely to be single-possession scores. But since losing teams are already less likely than winning teams to score at all, if we compare the share of offensive points that are single-possession scores, losing teams are automatically at a disadvantage. So instead, we must change the base from “all offensive points” to “all offensive holds”. Whereas previously we asked, “How often do teams score in a single possession?” we now ask: “When teams do score, how often do they score in one possession?”

Winning teams in the Men's and Women's divisions are somewhat more likely than losing teams to score offensive points in a single possession (by 5 and 8 percentage-points, respectively). Interestingly, in the Mixed division, winning teams are less likely to offensively hold with single-possession scores - by 7 percentage-points.
